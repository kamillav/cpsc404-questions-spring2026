## Week 10 – Dual reading Q  
**Shazif Ahmed**

In The book, Chapter 7 emphasizes that decision making authority in corporate settings flows through a clearly defined chain of command, where bypassing your manager  can maybe disrupt trust and organizational stability. In contrast, Producing Open Source Software explains that authority in open source projects is constrained by “forkability,” where contributors can exit and create alternative versions of the project if leadership decisions are unsatisfactory.

If forkability acts as an ultimate check on power, does open source governance actually create a more accountable form of leadership than corporate hierarchies, where employees cannot easily “fork” their organization? 
Or maybe does the lack of formal structure make decision-making more fragile, relying too heavily on informal consensus and social pressure?
